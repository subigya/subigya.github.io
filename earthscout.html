Case Study

Designing EarthScout’s Platform – Making Soil Data Work for Growers

When I first joined EarthScout, the challenge was clear: their sensors were powerful, but the software wasn’t keeping up. The company builds sensors and logic boards in the USA, ensuring reliability and quality, but the digital platform lagged behind the hardware’s potential.

From Redesign to Rebuild

We began with a simple redesign of their web and mobile applications. But as we dug deeper, it became clear that this wasn’t just a UI problem — we needed to rethink workflows, interactions, and scalability from the ground up.

Designing for Real Users

EarthScout’s users are farmers, agronomists, and researchers. They’re often in the field, in greenhouses, or in research plots — not behind a desk. Our design needed to be accessible, usable in tough environments, and able to deliver insights quickly.

Challenges in the Field

A major shift was moving from a hybrid mobile app to a native app with Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) support. This allowed growers to:

  • Activate devices in the field
  • Update firmware seamlessly
  • Sync data even with limited connectivity

This improved not just the experience, but also long-term maintainability.

Our Process

We applied an iterative design process:

  • Mapping workflows from device setup to data visualization
  • Creating low-fi sketches, then refining into hi-fi prototypes
  • Collaborating with stakeholders, engineers, and customer teams to ensure feature feasibility, alignment with grower needs, and smooth implementation across all platforms
  • Validating usability with actual growers

Impact in the Field

The result was a platform that finally matched the quality of EarthScout’s made in USA hardware. Growers and Customers reported better visibility into their soil, water, and weather conditions, leading to smarter irrigation and reduced costs. Researchers praised the ability to work with reliable historical datasets, improving decision-making.

Lessons I’ll Carry Forward

Designing for agriculture reinforced key principles:

  • Technology must adapt to the environment, not the other way around.
  • Hardware + software cohesion is critical — one without the other limits value.
  • Designing with scalability in mind enables growth without redesigning from scratch.
